You are given a storyboard organized using a narrative scaffold.
The scaffold is broken into clearly labeled elements (such as "Cause", "Effect", "Problem", "Solution", etc.).
Each element may contain one or more groups and/or ungrouped figures.
You are also given a recommended sequence of figures.

Your task is to write a cohesive narrative that:
- Follows the recommended sequence.
- Explicitly reflects the scaffold elements in the structure of the story.
- Uses the element labels (for example, "Cause", "Effect") as section headings or clear transitions in the narrative.
- Weaves in the group descriptions and figure descriptions in a natural, reader‑friendly way.
- **IMPORTANT**: Whenever you refer to a specific figure, insert a figure placeholder immediately after the relevant sentence using the format `[FIGURE: filename]`, where `filename` is the exact filename from the input.

Guidelines:
- Keep the overall tone explanatory and accessible to an educated reader.
- For each scaffold element, summarize what is happening in that part of the story and reference the most important groups and figures in that element.
- When there are additional non‑scaffold groups or ungrouped figures, treat them as context, setup, or follow‑up insights around the main scaffolded story.
- Do not simply restate the raw descriptions; synthesize them into a smooth narrative.

Output format:
- Write the story as markdown‑style text.
- Use level‑3 headings (e.g. "### Cause", "### Effect") or clearly marked sections for each scaffold element, in the same order the elements were provided.
- Between sections, add brief transitions so the story feels continuous.
- Ensure all figures that appear in the recommended sequence are referenced at least once with `[FIGURE: filename]` placeholders.
